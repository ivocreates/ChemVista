Chemistry Timeline

Ancient Times

Early metallurgy, alchemy, and chemical processes discovered by ancient civilizations.

1661 - Robert Boyle

Published "The Sceptical Chymist," distinguishing chemistry from alchemy.

1789 - Antoine Lavoisier

Published "Elementary Treatise on Chemistry," establishing modern chemical nomenclature.

1869 - Dmitri Mendeleev

Developed the periodic table, organizing elements by atomic weight and properties.

1898 - Marie & Pierre Curie

Discovered polonium and radium, advancing understanding of radioactivity.

1930s - Linus Pauling

Developed theories of chemical bonding and molecular structure.

Modern Era

Continued discoveries in nanotechnology, biochemistry, and materials science.

Nobel Prize in Chemistry

Notable Laureates

For the discovery of radium and polonium

Linus Pauling (1954)

For research on chemical bonding

Dorothy Hodgkin (1964)

For X-ray crystallography of biological molecules

For directed evolution of enzymes

Recent Breakthroughs
2023 - Quantum Dots

Moungi Bawendi, Louis Brus, Alexei Ekimov for quantum dot discovery and synthesis

2022 - Click Chemistry

Carolyn Bertozzi, Morten Meldal, Barry Sharpless for click chemistry and bioorthogonal reactions

2021 - Asymmetric Organocatalysis

Benjamin List and David MacMillan for asymmetric organocatalysis

Women in Chemistry

Marie Curie

First woman to win a Nobel Prize and the first person to win Nobel Prizes in two different scientific fields.

Rosalind Franklin

Her X-ray crystallography was crucial to understanding the double helix structure of DNA.

Dorothy Hodgkin

Pioneer in X-ray crystallography, determined structures of penicillin and vitamin B12.

Lise Meitner

Played a vital role in the discovery of nuclear fission, though initially overlooked for recognition.

Frances Arnold

Pioneer in directed evolution of enzymes, winner of 2018 Nobel Prize in Chemistry.

Jennifer Doudna

Co-developer of CRISPR gene editing technology, 2020 Nobel Prize winner.
