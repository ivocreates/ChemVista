Laboratory Safety

Essential safety guidelines and protocols for chemistry laboratories

Safety First: Always follow your institution's specific safety protocols

Emergency Procedures

Fire
  • Alert others immediately
  • Turn off gas valves if safe
  • Use appropriate fire extinguisher
  • Evacuate if fire spreads
Chemical Spill
  • Alert others in area
  • Contain spill if safe
  • Use spill kit materials
  • Report to supervisor
Injury
  • Get immediate help
  • Use eyewash/shower if needed
  • Remove contaminated clothing
  • Seek medical attention
Evacuation
  • Turn off equipment if safe
  • Close chemical containers
  • Exit via nearest route
  • Meet at assembly point

Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

Safety Goggles
  • Required: Always in lab
  • Type: Chemical splash protection
  • Fit: Snug against face
  • Care: Clean after each use
Regular glasses are NOT sufficient protection
Lab Coat
  • Material: Cotton or flame-resistant
  • Length: Below knees
  • Closure: Fully buttoned
  • Sleeves: Long, covering arms
Remove before leaving lab area
Gloves
  • Type: Chemical-resistant
  • Material: Nitrile, latex, or neoprene
  • Fit: Proper size, no tears
  • Use: Single-use when possible
Change between different chemicals
Footwear
  • Type: Closed-toe shoes
  • Material: Leather or synthetic
  • Height: Above ankle preferred
  • Sole: Non-slip, chemical-resistant
No sandals, flip-flops, or canvas shoes

Safety Equipment Location

Emergency Shower

For chemical splashes on body or clothing

How to Use:
  1. Pull activation handle
  2. Remove contaminated clothing
  3. Rinse for 15-20 minutes
  4. Seek medical attention
Eyewash Station

For chemical splashes in eyes

How to Use:
  1. Hold eyelids open
  2. Rinse both eyes for 15 minutes
  3. Roll eyes during rinsing
  4. Get immediate medical help
Fire Extinguisher

For small chemical fires

PASS Method:
  1. Pull the pin
  2. Aim at base of fire
  3. Squeeze handle
  4. Sweep side to side
Fume Hood

For containing and removing hazardous vapors

Best Practices:
  • Keep sash at proper height (18 inches)
  • Work at least 6 inches from sash
  • Don't block air flow with equipment
  • Clean up spills immediately
Spill Kit

For cleaning up chemical spills safely

Contents:
  • Absorbent materials
  • Neutralizing agents
  • Protective equipment
  • Disposal bags

Chemical Hazard Symbols

Explosive

Can explode under certain conditions

Flammable

Easily catches fire

Corrosive

Causes severe burns

Toxic

Poisonous if inhaled, ingested, or absorbed

Irritant

Causes skin or eye irritation

Environmental

Harmful to environment

Laboratory Do's

  • Always wear appropriate PPE
  • Read all labels and SDS sheets
  • Work in well-ventilated areas
  • Keep workspace clean and organized
  • Label all containers clearly
  • Wash hands frequently
  • Report accidents immediately
  • Use proper lifting techniques
  • Keep emergency numbers visible
  • Follow all written procedures

Laboratory Don'ts

  • Never eat or drink in the lab
  • Don't work alone with hazardous materials
  • Never pipette by mouth
  • Don't smell chemicals directly
  • Never mix chemicals without knowing the result
  • Don't pour chemicals down the drain
  • Never leave experiments unattended
  • Don't wear loose clothing or jewelry
  • Never ignore safety equipment
  • Don't take shortcuts with procedures

General Disposal Guidelines:
  • Never dispose of chemicals in regular trash
  • Label all waste containers with contents
  • Don't overfill containers
  • Separate incompatible chemicals
  • Use secondary containment for liquids
  • Close containers when not in use
  • Request pickup when containers are full
  • Keep waste inventory records
